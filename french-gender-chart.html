Masculin & Féminin

Every French noun is either masculine or feminine. The trick is to learn the patterns in the endings — and one short list of exceptions.

1

The words that signal gender

SituationMasculineFeminine
"the" (singular)le garçonla fille
"a / an" (singular)un livreune table
before a vowel or silent hl'hommel'eau
"the" / "some" (plural)les · desles · des

Note

In the plural, les and des are the same for both genders — so the gender "hides" in the plural. Always learn a noun in its singular form with le or la.

2

"Some / any" — du, de la, des

Use these when you mean an unspecified amount ("some bread", "some water"). The form depends on the gender of the noun — so it's another place gender shows up.

SituationFormExample
masculine noundu (= de + le)du pain  — some bread
feminine nounde lade la confiture  — some jam
before a vowel / silent hde l'de l'eau, de l'argent  — some water / money
plural (both genders)desdes fruits  — some fruit

The one big rule

After a negative, du / de la / des all collapse to de (or d' before a vowel):

Je mange du pain  →  Je ne mange pas de pain.

Il y a de la place  →  Il n'y a pas de place.

Note: du and des also mean "of the / from the" (le livre du prof = de + le). Same words, decided by the same gender logic.

3

Tell gender by the ending

Usually Masculine

le …

-agele fromage, le voyage (cheese, trip)
-mentle moment, le bâtiment
-eaule bateau, le château
-eule feu, le jeu (fire, game)
-ismele tourisme, le réalisme
-in / -onle jardin, le poisson
-oirle soir, le miroir
-ail / -eille travail, le soleil
Usually Feminine

la …

-tionla nation, la question (very reliable)
-sionla télévision, la décision
-téla liberté, la beauté
-ettela baguette, la fourchette
-ence / -ancela science, la chance
-urela voiture, la nature
-iela vie, la boulangerie
-elle / -illela poubelle, la famille

Watch the traps

A few common words break the rules — memorise these:

"-age" but feminine: la page la plage l'image

"-eau" but feminine: l'eau la peau  ·  "-on" but feminine: la maison la raison la saison

"-té / -ée" but masculine: le côté l'été le musée le lycée

5

How to remember it easily

1

Never learn a noun alone

Store the article with the word: not "table" but la table, not "livre" but le livre. The gender becomes part of the word in your memory.

2

Colour-code your vocabulary

Write masculine words in one colour and feminine words in another. Your eye learns the split before your brain does.

3

Learn the endings, not every word

The table above covers most nouns. If a word ends in -tion, -té, or -ette, bet feminine. If it ends in -age, -ment, or -eau, bet masculine.

4

Say it in a short phrase

Lock gender in with a tiny image: "un grand château", "une petite maison". The matching adjective reinforces the gender every time.

5

Memorise only the exceptions

You don't need to memorise thousands of words — just the short trap-list above. Everything else follows a pattern.
