# Daily Plan to A2 — 48 lessons (~8 weeks, 6 days/week)

This is sized by **content, not time.** Each day has a fixed set: new material +
revision + sentence practice. Finish fast or slow — the goal is to complete the
day's set. Lessons 1–8 vocabulary is already KNOWN (greetings, infinitive verbs,
food, countries, time words, family) and is used for revision only, never re-taught.

The tutor tracks "Current day" in progress.md and advances it by 1 each completed
lesson. Every lesson also drills due items from the review queue and ends with
3–5 English→French sentence translations.

---

## WEEK 1 — Present tense + numbers (turn known words into sentences)

**Day 1 — Subject pronouns + être**
- New: je/tu/il/elle/nous/vous/ils/elles; être (je suis, tu es, il est, nous sommes, vous êtes, ils sont). Numbers 0–10.
- Sentences: I am Iris · You are French · It is good · We are friends.

**Day 2 — avoir**
- New: avoir (j'ai, tu as, il a, nous avons, vous avez, ils ont). Numbers 11–20.
- Sentences: I have water · I have a friend · Do you have a menu? · She has a brother.

**Day 3 — aller + à/au/à la**
- New: aller (je vais, tu vas, il va…); à l'école, au restaurant, à la maison.
- Sentences: I go to school · We are going home · He goes to the restaurant.

**Day 4 — Regular -er verbs (present)**
- New: conjugate parler, aimer, manger, regarder, écouter (je parle, tu parles, il parle, nous parlons, vous parlez, ils parlent).
- Sentences: I speak French · You like coffee · We eat rice · They watch TV.

**Day 5 — Articles & gender**
- New: le/la/les, un/une/des; masculine vs feminine nouns; de + le = du.
- Sentences: The water is cold · A friend · The houses are big · I want a coffee.

**Day 6 — Questions + review**
- New: est-ce que, qu'est-ce que, où, combien, comment. Numbers 21–69.
- Sentences: Where is the toilet? · What do you want? · How much is it? · How are you?

---

## WEEK 2 — Negation, more verbs, futur proche

**Day 7 — Negation (ne… pas)**
- New: ne + verb + pas; je ne suis pas, je n'ai pas, je ne veux pas.
- Sentences: I am not French · I don't have water · She doesn't like tea.

**Day 8 — faire + prendre**
- New: faire (je fais, tu fais, il fait, nous faisons, vous faites, ils font); prendre (je prends…).
- Sentences: What do you do? · I take a coffee · We make dinner.

**Day 9 — Futur proche (je vais + infinitive)**
- New: aller + infinitive for near future.
- Sentences: I am going to eat · We are going to go home · He is going to buy bread.

**Day 10 — Days & time**
- New: lundi…dimanche; quelle heure est-il?; il est trois heures.
- Sentences: Today is Monday · It is two o'clock · See you tomorrow morning.

**Day 11 — Directions & places**
- New: à gauche, à droite, tout droit, en face, à côté de; au/à la/en + place.
- Sentences: Turn right · The school is on the left · It is next to the restaurant.

**Day 12 — Review + directions role-play**
- Revise weeks 1–2; mini dialogue asking and giving directions.
- Sentences: Excuse me, where is…? · Go straight then turn left.

---

## WEEK 3 — Food, shopping, passé composé (with avoir)

**Day 13 — Ordering food**
- New: je voudrais, l'addition s'il vous plaît, pour moi, comme entrée/plat.
- Sentences: I would like a coffee · The bill, please · For me, the noodles.

**Day 14 — Quantities & partitive**
- New: du/de la/des; un peu de, beaucoup de, une tasse de.
- Sentences: I want some water · A cup of tea · A lot of friends.

**Day 15 — Numbers 70–100 + prices**
- New: soixante-dix, quatre-vingts, quatre-vingt-dix, cent; ça coûte… euros.
- Sentences: It costs eighty euros · I have ninety-five · How much is the menu?

**Day 16 — Passé composé with avoir (-er verbs)**
- New: j'ai mangé, tu as parlé, il a regardé, nous avons aimé.
- Sentences: I ate rice · You spoke French · We watched TV yesterday.

**Day 17 — Passé composé irregular participles**
- New: fait, eu, été, pris, bu, vu (j'ai fait, j'ai pris…).
- Sentences: I had coffee · We made dinner · She took the menu · I saw a friend.

**Day 18 — Review + shopping role-play**
- Revise week 3; dialogue: ordering and paying at a café.
- Sentences: I would like two coffees · How much is it? · Here you go, thank you.

---

## WEEK 4 — Describing things & people

**Day 19 — Adjective agreement**
- New: grand/grande, petit/petite, bon/bonne, chaud/chaude, froid/froide.
- Sentences: The house is big · A small coffee · The food is good.

**Day 20 — Adjective placement + colours**
- New: most adjectives after the noun; colours (rouge, bleu, vert, noir, blanc); BAGS adjectives before.
- Sentences: A red car · The big house · A good little restaurant.

**Day 21 — Describing people & family**
- New: il/elle est + adjective; physical description (grand, petit, jeune, vieux).
- Sentences: My mother is tall · He is young · My friend is nice.

**Day 22 — Weather**
- New: il fait beau/chaud/froid; il pleut; quel temps fait-il?
- Sentences: It is hot today · It is raining · What's the weather like?

**Day 23 — Comparatives**
- New: plus … que, moins … que, aussi … que; meilleur.
- Sentences: Tea is better than coffee · She is taller than me · It is less cold today.

**Day 24 — Review + describe a photo**
- Revise week 4; describe a person and a place in 3–4 sentences.

---

## WEEK 5 — Past with être + daily routine

**Day 25 — Passé composé with être**
- New: movement verbs (aller, partir, venir, rester); je suis allé(e), elle est partie.
- Sentences: I went to school · She left this morning · We stayed home.

**Day 26 — Pronominal verbs**
- New: se lever, se coucher, s'appeler, se laver (je me lève, tu te lèves…).
- Sentences: I get up at seven · My name is Iris · He goes to bed late.

**Day 27 — Daily routine**
- New: sequence words (d'abord, ensuite, puis, après, enfin).
- Sentences: First I get up, then I eat · After, I go to work.

**Day 28 — Frequency**
- New: toujours, souvent, parfois, rarement, jamais.
- Sentences: I always drink coffee · She never eats meat · We sometimes go out.

**Day 29 — Review past + routine**
- Revise weeks 4–5; talk about a typical day.

**Day 30 — Storytelling**
- New: narrate yesterday with passé composé in 4–5 sentences.
- Sentences: Yesterday I got up early, ate, and went to school.

---

## WEEK 6 — Likes, plans, opinions

**Day 31 — Likes & dislikes**
- New: aimer/préférer/détester/adorer + infinitive or noun.
- Sentences: I like to read · I prefer tea · She hates the cold.

**Day 32 — Hobbies**
- New: hobby vocab (le sport, la musique, lire, voyager, cuisiner).
- Sentences: I like music · We like to travel · He plays football.

**Day 33 — Want / can / must**
- New: vouloir, pouvoir, devoir + infinitive (je veux, je peux, je dois).
- Sentences: I want to go · Can you help me? · I must work tomorrow.

**Day 34 — Suggestions & plans**
- New: on peut, on va, allons-y; faire des projets.
- Sentences: We can eat here · Let's go · We are going to travel.

**Day 35 — Opinions**
- New: je pense que, je trouve que, à mon avis, parce que.
- Sentences: I think it's good · In my opinion, French is nice · I'm tired because I worked.

**Day 36 — Review + plan a weekend**
- Revise week 6; describe weekend plans in 4–5 sentences.

---

## WEEK 7 — Connectors, pronouns, travel

**Day 37 — Connectors**
- New: et, mais, ou, donc, alors, parce que, puis.
- Sentences: I like coffee but I prefer tea · It's late so I'm going home.

**Day 38 — Object pronouns (light)**
- New: le/la/les (I see it → je le vois); placement before the verb.
- Sentences: I like it · I see her · We buy them.

**Day 39 — Travel & transport**
- New: le train, le bus, l'avion, la gare, l'aéroport, un billet; prendre le train.
- Sentences: I take the bus · Where is the station? · A ticket, please.

**Day 40 — Appointments & contact**
- New: un rendez-vous, réserver, l'adresse, le numéro; phone/email phrases.
- Sentences: I'd like to book a table · What's your number? · I have an appointment.

**Day 41 — Imperative**
- New: commands (parle/parlez, va/allez, prends/prenez); polite requests.
- Sentences: Listen! · Turn right · Please take a seat.

**Day 42 — Review + travel role-play**
- Revise week 7; dialogue: buying a ticket and asking the time.

---

## WEEK 8 — Consolidation + mock A2

**Day 43 — Tense review**
- Mix present, passé composé, futur proche in one set.
- Sentences: Yesterday I worked, today I rest, tomorrow I will travel.

**Day 44 — Vocabulary + numbers review**
- Theme sweep + prices/time/dates.

**Day 45 — Introduce yourself fully**
- Build a 6–8 sentence self-introduction (name, country, job, likes, plans).

**Day 46 — Role-plays**
- Café, hotel check-in, asking directions — combine everything.

**Day 47 — Mock A2 check**
- Short test against the A2 can-do list; note weak spots into the review queue.

**Day 48 — Final review + next goals**
- Revisit weakest items; celebrate; set goals beyond A2.
