Conjuguer les verbes

A French verb changes its ending to match who is doing the action and when. Learn the pronouns, the three regular families, and a handful of recipes — the rest follows.

Quick read

The endings are the colored part. Note -er and -re share -ons / -ez / -ent; the big giveaway of a -ir verb is the -iss- in the plural.

4

Three recipes for other times

Once you have the present, three formulas unlock the past, the ongoing past, and the future.

Passé composé — a completed past action ("I spoke / I have spoken")

avoir or être (present)  +  past participle   |   -er→é · -ir→i · -re→u
j'ai parlé · j'ai fini · j'ai vendu  — most verbs use avoir
je suis allé(e)  — verbs of movement/state use être, and the participle agrees

Imparfait — a background or habitual past ("I was speaking / I used to speak")

take the nous form, drop -ons, add   -ais · -ais · -ait · -ions · -iez · -aient
parlons → parl- → je parlais, nous parlions  (only être is irregular: j'étais)

Futur simple — a future action ("I will speak")

whole infinitive (-re drops its e)  +  -ai · -as · -a · -ons · -ez · -ont
parler → je parlerai · vendre → je vendrai  (irregular stems: être→ser-, avoir→aur-, aller→ir-, faire→fer-)

6

How to remember it easily

1

Master être and avoir before anything else

They are the two most common verbs and the helpers that build the past tense. Everything leans on them.

2

Learn endings as a chant

Say the six endings out loud in order: -e, -es, -e, -ons, -ez, -ent. Rhythm fixes them faster than reading.

3

One model verb per family

Know parler, finir, vendre cold. Any regular verb in that family copies the same pattern — just swap the stem.

4

Tenses are recipes, not new lists

Imparfait and futur reuse forms you already have (the nous-stem, the infinitive). Learn the formula once, apply everywhere.

5

Conjugate in real sentences

Don't drill in a vacuum — say "hier j'ai mangé, demain je mangerai". Tying tense to time-words makes it stick.
